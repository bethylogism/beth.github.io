About Me

I like practical people and theoretical problems.

As an infant, I tried to figure out what's really going on by studying the logical foundations of reality at Cambridge. I yielded to functionalism, per the rest of society. One side-effect of this was a brief attempt to figure out some inputs and outputs of the mind-brain, AKA "the reality machine". But without computationally modelling complex systems, that wasn't going to go anywhere suffciently interesting.

During some post-research time out, I accidentally grew a start-up from two of us in an attic into a successful, multi-million turnover, "Ed-tech" company. But, by then, the taste of technology had taken hold. It was clear that I could better combine everything interesting in life by programming.

So we return, once more, to functions. This time, with a little more creative control.
